Presentation

The Roman and Byzantine periods in Tunisia were marked by remarkable cultural and architectural achievements. Iconic cities such as Dougga and El Jem stand as enduring symbols of this era, renowned for their well-preserved ruins that showcase the grandeur of Roman urban planning, engineering, and artistic expression.

Principal Vestiges of the Roman and Byzantine Periods

Dougga (Thugga)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Dougga features well-preserved Roman ruins, including the Capitol, theater, and temples, showcasing the grandeur of Roman urban planning.

El Jem Amphitheater

One of the largest Roman amphitheaters in the world, this monumental structure, built in the 3rd century AD, reflects the architectural expertise of the Roman Empire.

Sbeitla (Sufetula)

Known for its well-preserved Roman forum, including three adjacent temples dedicated to Jupiter, Juno, and Minerva, and an impressive Byzantine basilica.

Bulla Regia

Renowned for its unique underground Roman villas designed to combat the region's heat, featuring intricate mosaics and well-preserved architecture.

Thuburbo Majus

A former Roman town with impressive remains of baths, a forum, and the Capitol, reflecting the prosperity of the region under Roman rule.

Byrsa Hill, Carthage

Includes Roman ruins and a Byzantine basilica, symbolizing the transition between the two eras.

Utique

One of the oldest cities in North Africa, Utique offers remnants of Punic and Roman history, including a Punic necropolis and Roman houses adorned with mosaics.

Chemtou

Known for its marble quarries and a Roman settlement, Chemtou showcases an ancient industrial site and a museum highlighting its historical significance.

Major Historical Events

Roman Period (146 BCE – 5th Century CE)

Roman Conquest of Carthage (146 BCE)

After the Third Punic War, Rome defeated and destroyed Carthage, incorporating the territory into the Roman Republic as the Province of Africa.

Establishment of Roman Africa (146 BCE)

The region became a critical breadbasket of the Roman Empire, supplying grain, olive oil, and other resources.

Construction of El Jem Amphitheater (3rd Century CE)

Built during the Severan dynasty, this amphitheater symbolized the prosperity and cultural significance of the province.

Byzantine Period (6th – 7th Century CE)

Byzantine Reconquest of Africa (534 CE)

Following the fall of the Vandals, Emperor Justinian's general, Belisarius, reconquered the region, re-establishing Byzantine control.

Construction of Byzantine Fortifications (6th Century CE)

The Byzantines fortified key cities like Carthage and built churches, such as those in Sbeitla, reflecting the Christian influence of the time.

Arrival of Islam and Fall of Byzantine Control (647 CE)

The Islamic invasion marked the end of Byzantine rule and the beginning of a new era in Tunisia's history.
